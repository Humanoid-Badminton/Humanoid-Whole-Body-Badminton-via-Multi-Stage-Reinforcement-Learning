Humanoid Whole-Body Badminton via Multi-Stage Reinforcement Learning

Chenhao Liu, Leyun Jiang, Yibo Wang, Kairan Yao, Jinchen Fu, Xiaoyu Ren
Beijing Phybot Technology Co., Ltd
arXiv Code

Abstract

Humanoid robots have demonstrated strong capability in interacting with static scenes across locomotion and manipulation, and even in unified loco-manipulation. Yet the real world is dynamic, and quasi-static interactions are insufficient. We present a reinforcement-learning–based training pipeline that produces a unified whole-body controller for humanoid badminton, enabling coordinated lower-body footwork and upper-body striking without any motion priors or expert demonstrations. Training follows a three-stage curriculum—first footwork acquisition, then precision-guided racket swing generation, and finally task-focused refinement—yielding motions in which both legs and arms serve the hitting objective. For deployment, we incorporate an Extended Kalman Filter (EKF) to estimate and predict shuttlecock trajectories for striking target. We also introduce a prediction-free variant that dispenses with EKF and explicit prediction, making the policy more end-to-end. We validate the framework through five experiments in simulation and the real world. In simulation, two robots sustain a rally of 21 consecutive hits. The prediction-free variant achieves successful hits with only limited degradation relative to the target-known policy. In real-world tests, both the prediction and the controller module exhibit high accuracy, and on-court hitting achieves an outgoing shuttle speed up to 10 m/s with a mean return landing distance of 3.5 m. The evidence demonstrates that our humanoid robot can deliver high-dynamic, high-precision performance in badminton and can be extended toward more demanding athletic domains.
